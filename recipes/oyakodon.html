Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a savory dish often served for breakfast containing eggs and chicken in a delicious broth served on top of a bowl of rice.

The name “Oyakodon” literally translates to “mother and child rice bowl”, which although morbid, it is still a very good dish.

Ingredients

  • White Rice
  • Chicken Thigh
  • Egg
  • Onion
  • Dashi Stock
  • Mirin
  • Soy Sauce
  • Sugar
  • Optional: Shiso Leaf

Steps

  1. Put your white rice with some water into a rice cooker and begin cooking the rice
  2. Chop your chicken thigh into bite sized pieces, and slice your onion, making sure to cut with the grain of the onion
  3. In a small pan, mix together your dashi stock, mirin, soy sauce, and sugar
  4. Place the cut chicken and onion into the pan
  5. Simmer the mixture on medium-high until chicken is fully cooked and onions are soft
  6. Beat one egg in a bowl
  7. Pour egg over the mixture, making sure to get an even coverage
  8. Cover the pan and cook on high heat for a minute or so until the egg is around 90% cooked through
  9. In a bowl, put in a serving of rice, and place the chicken, onion, and egg mixture on top of the rice along with the remaining broth
  10. Optionally: garnish with some shiso leaf
  11. Enjoy!
